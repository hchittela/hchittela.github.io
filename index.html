Hemanth Chittela

Hey! I'm Hemanth, a CS student at UPenn by day, fun-loving enthusiast by night. As a student I get to pursue my interests and learn from those around me – exactly what I love doing. I'm still exploring the CS world and am currently focusing on robotics. You can find more about me below and reach me through the contact form or via the links at the top of the page.

The story.

Raised in the midwest and now studying in the city of Philly. I'm a builder. A tinkerer. A hands-on kinda guy.

My story started out with the “played with Legos since I was a kid" situation. Over the years however, the piles of bolts and raw materials in my room receded to give way to electronics and books on coding. I'm now captivated and enthused by software and computer science.  I've found a bit of a niche for myself, studying both computer science and cognitive science to focus on their interaction and application to artificial intelligence (robotics, machine learning, and natural language processing are a few interests).

From a technical standpoint, I’m adept at object-oriented programming (Java and C#), comfortable with functional programming (OCaml), and have used the ASP.NET MVC framework in conjunction with SQL databases. It's all detailed in my resume (over to the right).

Aside from a rigorous course load, on campus I'm involved with an aerospace and an aerial robotics club (we’re trying swarm programming!) and am working as a research assistant in a biologically-inspired robotics lab.

I love a good challenge and strive to keep things interesting.
